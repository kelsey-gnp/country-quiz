The Wanderer

Singapore

You've probably traveled a fair amount already and you want something different, but still safe and modern. The other options on this list just haven't felt like a fit, and you can't always explain why.

Your strength

You already know the standard bedside-abroad path isn't your path, and you're not forcing yourself into it just because it's the popular option. You're not working within the standard playbook that many nursing professionals default to, and it actually fits your background.

Your blind spot

The order is flipped here, and most people don't realize it until it trips them up. For most countries, you sort licensure first, then find a job. In Singapore, it's job first, then the employer sponsors the rest. This pathway depends a lot more on relationships and networking than paperwork.

This week

Start mapping your skills and credentials and possible connections you can leverage to explore potential options. Look for who's working in the roles you want, and start thinking about how to get in front of them.

My Move Abroad Strategy Sessions help you map out what's realistically possible for your specific background and what direction to go first before you sink months of research into non-viable pathways.

The Field Worker

Global Health / Humanitarian Track

You know there's real suffering in the world, and you've got the skills and the drive to actually help. You just don't know how to turn that into something sustainable. And you want to do it ethically, not performatively. As long as you're having an impact that's bigger than you as an individual and putting some good into the world, you've done your job.

Your strength

You're not chasing a two-week feel-good trip. You're comfortable with being uncomfortable. You want to connect with your inner purpose, not a paycheck. If someone handed you a plane ticket tomorrow and told you to pack your bags, you'd take it!

Your blind spot

Short medical missions feel like the obvious entry point, but they're not always sustainable for you or the community you're serving. It's a marathon, not a sprint, and it takes real relationship-building and a long view, not a quick fix. The key is working with an organization that aligns with your values and can provide the support, housing, and structure you need to make it sustainable for you as a whole person.

This week

Research two or three organizations doing the type of work you're drawn to, and look at what credentials and experience they require to see where you land.

A strategy session helps you figure out how to participate in global health without burning out. It can run in parallel with another path if you're not ready to choose just one, and helps you figure out where to focus your energy — which organizations, connections, and extra trainings will actually move the needle for you.
